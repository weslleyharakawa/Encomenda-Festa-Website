Traditional and regional Brazilian dishes
From Encomenda Festa you can feel the tast of Brazil in their traditional dishes like:

Feijoada
Reputedly introduced in Brazil by black slaves as early as the sixteenth century, "feijoada" -- a bean potpourri type dish. It is without a doubt the country's national dish. It is in fact so popular throughout Brazil that most restaurants, from the humblest inner-city " lanchonete" to the sophisticated hotels lining the Bahia and Rio shores, designate specific days in which "feijoada" is served at lunch time as "o prato do dia" -- the day's special. Other eateries pride themselves in the fact that they serve it every day and advertise it in the yellow pages and/or in conspicuous blackboards outside their premises.

Picanha na Chapa
Unique Brazilian cut rump steak with cassava, chips, rice and Brazilian salad.

Vatapá
One of the classic dishes of Brazilian cuisine. It hails from Bahia state on the northeast coast where many of the dishes have African origins. Made with shrimp, coconut milk, palm oil (Azeite de dende) and cashews nuts, mashed into a creamy paste.

Bobó de Camarão
It is a traditional soup from Northern Brazil, made with mash cassava, prawns, coconut milk, Mashed cassava with shrimps fried in palm oil (azeite de dende) and onions, coconut milk. Served with white rice.
MOQUECA DE PEIXE
The most traditional dish from Bahia and Espirito Santo. Surd fish cooked in green, red peper and tomato sauce, finalized with fresh coriander and spice olive oil.

Moqueca Mista
Prawns and Surd fish cooked in green, red peper and tomato sauce, finalized with fresh coriander and spice olive oil.

Peixe ao Molho de Camarão
Fine fish filet grilled and cooked with prawns sauce, served with rice or mash potato.

Caldeirada de Frutos do Mar
Fish, prawns, calamari fried in garlic sauce and white wine. Cooked in Brazilian style.

Peito de Frango Grelhado
Chicken breast grilled in butter with creamily mustard sauce.
